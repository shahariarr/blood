Services

Our organization provides critical services related to the collection, testing, processing, storage, and distribution of blood and blood products. These services play a crucial role in supporting healthcare systems and saving lives. Here are some of the key services typically offered by blood bank organizations:

Blood Donation

Our organization organize blood donation drives and encourage voluntary blood donation from healthy individuals. They collect whole blood or specific blood components like red blood cells, platelets, and plasma.

Blood Testing

After collection, the donated blood is subjected to rigorous testing to detect infectious diseases and other health conditions. This is essential to ensure the safety of both the donors and the recipients.

Blood Storage

Our organization have specialized facilities for the proper storage and preservation of blood and blood products to maintain their viability until they are needed for transfusion or medical treatments.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQS ABOUT BLOOD AND BLOOD DONATION.

  • How much blood is taken?

    For a whole blood donation, approximately 0.5 L of blood is collected. For donations of other blood products, such as platelet or plasma, the amount collected depends on your height, weight and platelet count.

  • What is the most common blood to donate?

    The list below shows the percentage of donors with each blood type:
    O positive: 35%
    O negative: 13%
    A positive: 30%
    A negative: 8%
    B positive: 8%
    B negative: 2%
    AB positive: 2%
    AB negative: 1%

  • How frequently can you donate blood?

    every 56 days (or two months) for whole blood donations, which totals six times a year.

  • What are the 3 rarest blood types?

    The rarest blood types are:
    B negative(B -ve), which is found in 1.5 percent of the total population.
    AB negative(AB -ve), which is found in 0.6 percent of the total population.
    AB positive(AB +ve), which is found in 3.4 percent of the total population.

  • What are the side effects of giving blood?

    Some people may feel nauseous, lightheaded, or dizzy after donating blood. If this happens, it should only last a few minutes. You can lie down with your feet up at the until you feel better. You may also experience some bleeding at the site of the needle.
